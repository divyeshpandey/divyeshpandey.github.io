Reco — Fake News Detector

A case study on how can we break the chain of fake news on an instant messaging app.

Divyesh Pandey

Problem Statement

The messaging app can be a misinformation highway in its own right. The same factors that make sending messages and images so easy, also make it easy for people to pass along shocking or emotionally charged hearsay, rumors and also fake news. In the world of AI, miscreants are finding newer ways to propagate this misinformation.

Some of these forwards include (but not limited to)

  • Unfounded claims of DIY medical remedies
  • Fake news for political agendas
  • False information that can spread panic among people

Dot Vote

This is one of the common methods. Dot Vote is a Design Sprint method to achieve group consensus around a single idea to address the Design Sprint focus. Before voting begins, review the criteria for selecting an idea to prototype. It’s helpful to remind the team of the business problem, goal, and deliverables, as well as success metrics and sprint questions. If a clear consensus can’t be achieved, use the other methods provided in this section.

Decision Matrix

A Decision Matrix is a Design Sprint method that helps a team evaluate ideas based on a set of specific criteria most useful to the goals of the Sprint. This is a useful method to deploy when the team is unable to reach a clear consensus with a Dot Vote. The matrix is a simple table or diagram and most commonly compares user impact versus implementation effort. Ideally, you want ideas that are either high impact/low effort or high impact/high effort.

Heatmap Voting

In this method, team members get an unlimited amount of smaller dots and can use as many dots as they want to vote on specific features in all the Solution Sketches, versus voting for an entire sketch. The intention is to create a heatmap on the sketches that shows which features are worth prototyping. This is generally followed by a discussion of the features and then a round of prioritization voting.

Push / Pull Design decision:

  1. The keypad is basically a push model. When we design to influence our users through a push strategy, we begin by forecasting their needs and then designing the most efficient system to ensure everything our users need is available at the right time and the right place
  2. Reco App is actually a pull concept.

Going from push to pull

  1. The art of it all is going from push to pull. It’s striving for delivering value to the end-user when he or she pulls it rather than trying to push things that most likely are irrelevant.
  2. Utilizing pull is trying to find an intrinsic way to motivate people to act. To motivate people to have a purpose or end-goal. Going from push to pull is moving the burden to the users: letting the users take action instead of you. Going from push to pull is easing the cognitive load on the user and letting the user explore — access, attract, and achieve rather than being instructed.

Why, How, Prototype, Iterate (WHPI)

The key idea behind WHPI is to timebox the creation of an initial prototype for any deliverable while evaluating that prototype against a clear and agreed-upon set of desired outcomes. WHPI encourages an outcome-first approach to prototyping that prioritizes the result of the prototype over the form of the prototype. It is equally accessible and applicable to any situation which calls for the creation of a deliverable.

Usability Study

The Usability Study is a Design Sprint method to identify any usability issues and determine the user’s satisfaction with the product prototype. This involves observing the user attempting to complete a task or set of tasks while using your product. Typically, the user is presented with different scenarios and asked to think aloud as they try to complete the tasks.

Examples of questions this method can answer:

  • Does the UI give enough information to start a specific task?
  • How do users perform tasks on a new UI?
  • Is an element discoverable?

Final Proposed Solution: MVP

RECO KEYPAD

To come up with Keypad as a final proposed solution for fake news validation experience for all age group users. The design decision depends on the below rationale :

  • It should be accessible to all
  • It should be inclusive
  • It should have push rather than pull for now.
  • Business goal
  • Users need as first priority to check whether the news is real or fake.
  • Incremental design innovation. Validate the need.
  • Tech behind the solution.

This is the final proposed solution to fight against fake news. This is the MVP. The features are :

  1. User installs the Reco Keypad App from AppStore
  2. User can activate the Reco keypad as default keypad for mobile
  3. Open WhatsApp App
  4. Tap to keyboard
  5. Tap to validate any news/images/Videos from any group forwarded by a friend
  6. Reco keypad would show the sentiment meter in true/false
  7. If Reco finds that news is 100% fake or true. It would the verdict as validated.
  8. If not it would show in % format and ask for user’s vote to help invalidation
  9. Users can share forward back to inform others.
  10. User can read more about the news with attached references by Reco
  11. Reco would learn from the user’s vote and build a database for fake news once entered into the system.
  12. Reco would use AI as advanced tech to validate the news.
  13. Reco is in another local language as well.
  14. User can deactivate the Reco keypad and use the native one
  15. Users can customized apps as separate.
